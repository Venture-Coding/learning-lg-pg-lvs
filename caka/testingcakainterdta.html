Description

When a customer (i.e. Our Insured or Not at fault Third Party) makes a new claim we must ensure we are not only obtaining all relevant information from them, but also provide them with certain information in order to meet our obligations under the General Insurance Code of Practice (GICOP).

When making a claim we must tell the customer:

  • about our claims process
  • about any excess amount they have to pay in relation to their claim
  • about any waiting or no cover periods that need to finish before we start paying them under their policy
  • how to contact us regarding their claim

They will assist the Claim Owner in completing validation checks on a claim/policy.

Key points can be noted on the claim and policy to ensure the correct coverage and assistance is provided to the customer through the life of the claim.

A claim validation must be completed by Claims within one business day of lodgement (phone or digital). This is a series of checks designed to determine claim coverage and any further actions required on the claim (if any).
 
  1. Go to Actions
  2. Select New Note
  3. Within the New Note box select Use Template
  4. Select the most appropriate in the Topic Field (as per table below)
  5. Select the most appropriate in the Type Field (as per table below)
  6. Select the most appropriate in the Line of Business Field (as per table below)
  7. Select the appropriate template
Part of validating a claim is ensuring Policy Obligations have been met. Policy Obligations include:
  • Our Insured is 100% At Fault for the accident
  • There is no current cross claim with IAG (the Other Party hasn't lodged a claim with their own NRMA, SGIO, SGIC, RACV or CGU policy)
  • Claims that are not part of the Retained Customer Agreement (RCA)
  • Our Insured is allocated in the system to a repairer. This provides some assurance that Our Insured's claim will proceed, and the excess will be paid
  • If Our Insured is using a 'Cash Settle Repairer' the assessment must be recorded in ClaimCenter to provide assurance that Our Insured's claim will proceed, and the excess will be paid
  • Our Insured is allocated to a salvage centre/assessment centre
  • Third Party Damage Only claims - The excess must be paid in full, or we have reached an agreement with Our Insured for an excess payment plan

Storm Storm General:
  • What damage has been sustained?

  • Is there damage to a fence? If so, is this fence shared with a neighbour?

  • What colour was the water?
  • How high did the water come up?

  • What is the type of roof damaged? (ie. Polycarb, tiles, corrugated iron, asbestos?)
  • Are you aware of any pre-existing damage to your roof?
  • Is there any water staining or ceiling damage?

  • Has the damage exposed any asbestos, sewage or wastewater, dangerous chemicals, or mould?
  • Is there damage to a fence? If so, is this fence shared with a neighbour?
  • Is there any carpet / flooring damage? Is the carpet still wet?
Theft Theft whilst at the situation:
  • Who else has access to your property?
  • Who owns the item?
  • Who was the last person to leave your property and when (date and time)?
  • Is there evidence of forced entry and / or exit?
  • Is the property secure?
  • Was the property tenanted at the time and was the theft caused by tenant?
  • Do you have CCTV / security camera footage?
Theft of item whilst away from the situation:
  • Who owns the item?
  • Do you know who stole the item?
  • When was the last time you saw the item?

WHAT HAS HAPPENED:
  • Summarise what damage has occurred per the conversation had with the Customer/Broker.
  • ie. Overnight during a storm there was a lightning strike to the property which has damaged the building and some manufacturing machinery.
WHAT IS THE EXTENT OF THE LOSS, WHAT ARE THEY CLAIMING FOR AND HOW DOES THE POLICY RESPOND INCLUDING ADDITIONAL BENEFITS:
  • List what building, general item/content, financial loss they are claiming for.
  • Example Damage to 3 x industrial baking ovens, 4 x mixers, 2 x freezers and 1 cool room. Water damage to shopfront ceiling. Business unable to trade and claiming loss of profit and wages.
  • Review loss details, PDS (Product Disclosure Statement), and Certificate of Insurance (Certificate)
  • Is the event covered? (For Flood: Is there cover? – if not, set Claim Decision Status Pending). Click here for Contentious Claims information.
  • Is the item/property covered and listed on the Certificate?
  • Any additional benefits to consider?
  • Any sub limits?
  • Any exclusions that may apply?
  • What further information do we need to confirm coverage?
  • Have you considered any state specific conditions? (ie. Real estate regulations and conditions concerning the definition of item)
  • How many excesses apply?
  • Have you used CASI for assistance?
WHAT WAS DISCUSSED AND ACTIONED:
  • Summarise who you spoke to and what you spoke to them about including any time frames and when they are to expect our next contact.
  • Summarise actions taken on the claim making sure to utilise all claim centre fields (ie. GST information, EFT information, reserving etc.)
WHAT ARE THE NEXT STEPS INCLUDING OUTSTANDING DOCUMENTS:
  • List outstanding actions in order to progress the claim
  • List outstanding documents/information in order to progress the claim
  • Note when the Customer/Broker expects to hear from us next (ie. Contact Customer when Loss Adjuster report in)
